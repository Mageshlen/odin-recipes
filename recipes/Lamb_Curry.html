Lamb Curry

Description:

Lamb curry is a rich, flavorful dish that features tender pieces of lamb simmered in a spicy, aromatic gravy.The base of the curry often includes onions, garlic, and ginger, which are sautéed to develop a deep, savory foundation.The result is a comforting, hearty meal with a perfect balance of spicy, savory.

Ingredients:

For the Curry:
  1. Lamb: 1.5 to 2 pounds of lamb, cut into bite-sized chunks (shoulder or leg works well)
  2. Onion: 1 large onion, finely chopped
  3. Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced
  4. Ginger: 1 tablespoon, minced or grated
  5. Tomatoes: 2 medium tomatoes, chopped, or 1 can of diced tomatoes
  6. Yogurt: 1/2 cup (optional, for a richer, creamier sauce)
  7. Cooking Oil: 2-3 tablespoons (vegetable oil, ghee, or coconut oil)
  8. Salt: to taste
  9. Fresh Cilantro: for garnish
  10. Potatoes: 1-2 medium, peeled and cut into chunks
Spices:
  1. Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
  2. Coriander Powder: 2 teaspoons
  3. Turmeric Powder: 1 teaspoon
  4. Garam Masala: 1-2 teaspoons
  5. Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  6. Paprika: 1 teaspoon (optional, for color)
  7. Cinnamon Stick: 1 small piece
  8. Cardamom Pods: 2-3
  9. Cloves: 3-4

Steps:

  1. Prepare Ingredients:
    2.
    • Chop the onion, garlic, and ginger. Cut the lamb into chunks if not already done.
    • Chop tomatoes or have canned tomatoes ready.
  2. Brown the Lamb:
    3.
    • Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
    • Add the lamb chunks in batches, searing them until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Cook Aromatics:
    4.
    • In the same pot, add the cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown, stirring occasionally (about 8-10 minutes).
  4. Add Garlic and Ginger:
    5.
    • Add the minced garlic and ginger. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add Spices:
    6.
    • Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, chili powder, paprika (if using), and salt. Stir well to combine with the onions, garlic, and ginger.
  6. Incorporate Tomatoes:
    7.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes or canned tomatoes to the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  7. Combine Lamb and Simmer:
    8.
    • Return the browned lamb to the pot. Stir to coat the lamb with the spice mixture.
    • Add enough water to cover the lamb (about 2 cups). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  8. Simmer:
    9.
    • Cover and simmer on low heat for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the lamb is tender. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to maintain the desired consistency.
  9. Optional Additions:
    10.
    • If using potatoes and carrots, add them about 30 minutes before the curry is done, so they cook through but remain firm.
    • Add peas in the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  10. Finish the gravy:
    11.
    • Stir in the yogurt (if using) and garam masala. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
    • Adjust salt and spices as needed.
  11. Garnish and Serve:
    12.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro.
    • Serve hot with rice and naan or roti.
Enjoy your flavorful and hearty lamb curry!
